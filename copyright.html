Copyright in Graphic Design (Jamaica)

Copyright is a form of legal protection granted to creators of original works. In graphic design, it safeguards designs, images, logos, and other artistic creations from being copied, distributed, or used without permission.

Legal Framework

  • Governed by the Copyright Act (Act No. 5 of 1993, amended in 2015).
  • Protects literary, musical, dramatic, and artistic works, including graphic designs.
  • Duration: Life of the creator + 95 years (depending on type of work).
  • Rights: Economic rights (income from work) and moral rights (credit and protection against misuse).

Importance for Designers

  • Protects logos and brand identity from unauthorized use.
  • Prevents copying of artwork, ensuring originality is respected.
  • Secures income by allowing licensing or selling of work.
  • Establishes professional credibility by recognizing the designer as the rightful author.
