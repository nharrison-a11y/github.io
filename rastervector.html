Raster vs Vector Graphics

Raster graphics are pixel‑based images, perfect for photos and detailed artwork. They capture subtle colors and shading but are resolution‑dependent — enlarging them can cause blurriness or pixelation. Common formats include JPEG, PNG, GIF, BMP, edited in tools like Photopea, Photoshop, or GIMP.

Vector graphics use mathematical paths (points, lines, curves) instead of pixels. They are resolution‑independent, meaning they stay sharp at any size. Vectors are ideal for logos, icons, and illustrations, and are saved as SVG, AI, EPS, PDF using software like Illustrator, CorelDRAW, or Inkscape.
